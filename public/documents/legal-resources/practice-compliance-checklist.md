## A Comprehensive Guide for Australian Medical Practices

## How to Use This Checklist

This compliance checklist is designed to help medical practice owners, practice managers, and healthcare professionals identify and address key regulatory obligations. Review each section and mark items as complete, in progress, or requiring attention.

**Legend:**
- ☐ Not yet addressed
- ☐ In progress
- ☐ Complete

---

## 1. Professional Registration and Credentials

### AHPRA Registration

☐ All practitioners hold current registration with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA)

☐ Registration details are publicly displayed as required

☐ Annual registration renewal processes are documented and calendared

☐ Continuing Professional Development (CPD) requirements are being met

☐ Practitioner registration conditions (if any) are documented and complied with

☐ Locum and visiting practitioners' registration status is verified before engagement

### Medicare Provider Numbers

☐ All eligible practitioners hold current Medicare provider numbers

☐ Provider number applications for new practitioners are processed promptly

☐ Billing practices align with Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) requirements

☐ Bulk billing policies are clearly documented and communicated to patients

---

## 2. Privacy and Information Management

### Privacy Act Compliance

☐ Practice has a current Privacy Policy that complies with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)

☐ Privacy Policy is readily accessible to patients (displayed in practice and on website)

☐ Privacy Collection Statement is provided to patients at or before the time of collection

☐ Staff have received training on privacy obligations and the handling of personal information

☐ Procedures exist for responding to access and correction requests within required timeframes

### Health Records Management

☐ Patient health records are maintained in accordance with state/territory health records legislation

☐ Records retention periods comply with legal requirements (minimum 7 years for adults; until age 25 for children)

☐ Electronic health records systems meet security and backup requirements

☐ My Health Record participation and upload procedures are documented

☐ Processes exist for secure destruction of records after retention periods expire

### Data Breach Response

☐ Notifiable Data Breaches scheme obligations are understood

☐ Data breach response plan is documented and staff are trained

☐ Contact details for the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) are accessible

---

## 3. Workplace Health and Safety

### General WHS Obligations

☐ Practice is registered with the relevant state/territory WHS regulator

☐ WHS policy is current and communicated to all workers

☐ Risk assessments have been conducted for all work activities

☐ Incident reporting procedures are in place and understood by staff

☐ Workers' compensation insurance is current and adequate

### Infection Prevention and Control

☐ Infection control policy aligns with NHMRC Australian Guidelines for the Prevention and Control of Infection in Healthcare

☐ Hand hygiene facilities and products are readily available

☐ Personal protective equipment (PPE) is provided and training has been given on correct use

☐ Sharps disposal procedures comply with relevant standards

☐ Cleaning and sterilisation protocols are documented and followed

☐ Vaccination policy for staff is in place (including influenza and COVID-19 as applicable)

### Emergency Preparedness

☐ Emergency evacuation plan is current and displayed

☐ Fire safety equipment is maintained and inspected as required

☐ First aid kits are stocked and accessible

☐ Medical emergency response protocols are documented

☐ Staff are trained in emergency procedures including CPR

---

## 4. Employment and Engagement

### Employment Compliance

☐ Employment contracts are in writing and comply with the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)

☐ Correct Award coverage has been identified (Health Professionals and Support Services Award, Nurses Award, etc.)

☐ Pay rates meet or exceed Award minimum rates

☐ Superannuation contributions are made in accordance with the Superannuation Guarantee

☐ Leave entitlements are correctly calculated and provided

☐ Payslips are issued within one working day of payment

### Contractor Arrangements

☐ Independent contractor arrangements are properly structured and documented

☐ Sham contracting risks have been assessed

☐ Service agreements address key terms including fees, GST, insurance, and termination

☐ Contractor ABNs have been verified

☐ Superannuation obligations for contractors have been assessed

### Workplace Policies

☐ Anti-discrimination and harassment policy is in place

☐ Bullying prevention policy complies with Safe Work Australia guidelines

☐ Social media and communications policy addresses professional obligations

☐ Dress code and presentation standards are documented

---

## 5. Medicare and Billing Compliance

### Billing Practices

☐ Billing procedures comply with the Health Insurance Act 1973 (Cth)

☐ MBS item number selection processes are documented and auditable

☐ Informed financial consent is obtained for private fees

☐ Patient accounts and receipts meet prescribed requirements

☐ Third-party billing arrangements (if any) comply with assignment of benefit rules

### Compliance Systems

☐ Internal audits of Medicare billing are conducted regularly

☐ Staff involved in billing have received appropriate training

☐ Compliance concerns are documented and escalated appropriately

☐ Professional Services Review (PSR) scheme is understood

☐ Record keeping supports Medicare claims made

---

## 6. Professional Indemnity and Insurance

### Insurance Coverage

☐ Professional indemnity insurance is current and adequate for all practitioners

☐ Public liability insurance is in place

☐ Workers' compensation insurance is current

☐ Business insurance (property, equipment, business interruption) is adequate

☐ Cyber liability insurance has been considered

### Claims Management

☐ Insurance notification obligations are understood

☐ Process exists for prompt notification of potential claims or incidents

☐ Insurance certificates are retained and renewal dates calendared

---

## 7. Advertising and Marketing

### AHPRA Advertising Guidelines

☐ All advertising complies with the National Law and AHPRA guidelines

☐ No use of testimonials in advertising

☐ Qualifications and titles used are accurate and not misleading

☐ Before and after images (if used) comply with guidelines

☐ Social media content has been reviewed for compliance

### Website Compliance

☐ Website includes required privacy policy

☐ Terms and conditions are appropriate for any online services

☐ Practitioner profiles are accurate and current

☐ Online booking systems comply with consent requirements

---

## 8. Medicines and Therapeutic Goods

### Prescribing and Dispensing

☐ Prescribing practices comply with state/territory requirements

☐ Prescription records are maintained as required

☐ Schedule 8 (controlled substances) handling procedures are documented

☐ Drug storage meets Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) requirements

☐ Sample medication handling procedures are in place

### Medical Devices

☐ Medical devices used are registered on the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG)

☐ Device maintenance and calibration records are maintained

☐ Adverse event reporting procedures are understood

---

## 9. Telehealth and Digital Health

### Telehealth Services

☐ Telehealth services comply with AHPRA and Medical Board guidelines

☐ Informed consent processes address telehealth-specific matters

☐ Technology platforms meet privacy and security requirements

☐ Medicare telehealth billing requirements are understood and followed

☐ Prescribing via telehealth follows regulatory requirements

### Digital Health Security

☐ Cybersecurity measures are in place and regularly reviewed

☐ Multi-factor authentication is used for sensitive systems

☐ Staff have received cybersecurity awareness training

☐ Business continuity plans address IT system failures

---

## 10. Mandatory Reporting and Notifications

### Mandatory Notifications

☐ Mandatory reporting obligations under the National Law are understood

☐ Processes exist for making notifications to AHPRA where required

☐ Child protection mandatory reporting obligations (state/territory specific) are understood

☐ Elder abuse reporting obligations are understood

### Coronial and Legal Obligations

☐ Processes exist for responding to coronial investigations

☐ Subpoena and court order response procedures are documented

☐ Legal professional privilege considerations are understood

---

## 11. Corporate Governance

### Business Structure Compliance

☐ Business structure complies with corporate practice rules (state/territory specific)

☐ Company (if applicable) complies with Corporations Act requirements

☐ Trust (if applicable) is administered in accordance with trust deed

☐ Partnership agreement (if applicable) is current

### Regulatory Registrations

☐ ABN is current and registered with the Australian Business Register

☐ GST registration (if applicable) is current

☐ Business name registration is current

☐ Relevant state/territory business licences are maintained

---

## 12. Quality and Accreditation

### Practice Accreditation

☐ Practice accreditation status is current (RACGP, AGPAL, QPA, or equivalent)

☐ Accreditation standards are integrated into practice policies

☐ Quality improvement activities are documented

☐ Patient feedback mechanisms are in place

### Clinical Governance

☐ Clinical governance framework is documented

☐ Clinical incident review processes exist

☐ Peer review or quality assurance activities occur regularly

☐ Evidence-based clinical guidelines inform practice
